External compressible flow study

Back to projects

This CFD study analyzes external compressible flow over a trapezoidal wing in Ansys, starting from a baseline case at Mach 0.8395 and 3.06 degrees angle of attack and extending into angle-of-attack and Mach sweeps. The report computes aerodynamic reference quantities, transforms forces from body axes to wind axes, and tracks how lift, drag, and pitching moment change as flow conditions vary. Across the sweeps, the results show lift increasing with angle of attack while drag becomes increasingly dominated by pressure effects, and they highlight how higher-Mach conditions amplify compressibility effects and sharply increase total drag.
