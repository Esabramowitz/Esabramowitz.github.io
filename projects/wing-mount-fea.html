Wing mount structural analysis

Finite element analysis of a 3D-printed wing mount used to attach wings to a carbon-fiber square-tube fuselage in a modular RC aircraft design. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Open PDF Back to projects

Goal

Ensure the wing mount can withstand expected aerodynamic forces during flight, since failure would be critical. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Tools

  • CAD + FEA workflow (SolidWorks/Ansys-style)
  • Meshing + convergence checks
  • Material modeling assumptions

Setup

  • Fixed constraint at the fuselage slot bottom; additional slot boundaries prevent inward motion while allowing sliding. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Loads derived from lift and drag coefficients and wing geometry, with multiple reaction-force cases explored. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
  • Primary failure metric: first principal stress, treating PLA as brittle. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Material assumptions

PLA modeled as linear isotropic (noting 3D-printed parts are not truly isotropic); tensile strength listed around 50 MPa. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Mesh confidence

Performed a mesh study to check that peak stress locations were consistent and not singularities. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}

Results snapshot

Stress results were reported across multiple loading “cases” and velocities; some conditions exceed the assumed brittle strength threshold, motivating physical validation testing. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

Next step: clamp + pull test with a force gauge to validate simulation before redesigning. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}
