Analysis of Competition RC Aircraft

Back to projects

This CFD project analyzes the aerodynamic performance of Columbia University Airplane Club’s competition RC aircraft using ANSYS Fluent. The study builds a full-aircraft CFD workflow from CAD and meshing through steady-state RANS simulation, then evaluates lift, drag, pitching moment, and flow behavior across a sweep of angles of attack. The results identify stall onset between about 12 and 14 degrees, show peak aerodynamic efficiency near 0 degrees angle of attack, and provide design insight for future iterations, including tail configuration and wingtip modifications.
